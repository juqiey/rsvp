Abstract— This paper documents a wedding event in Kuala Terengganu, Malaysia, presenting a web-based RSVP system and a companion guestbook to capture attendance status and personalized messages from attendees interactively. The system design is presented within the framework of a standard academic submission style.
Keywords— wedding, RSVP, guestbook, interactive system, IEEE style
INTRODUCTION

Marriage represents a major milestone in human life, symbolizing commitment and shared purpose between two individuals. This paper documents a wedding ceremony scheduled for 20 July 2025 at Dewan Seri Harmoni, Kuala Terengganu. In addition to event documentation, a web-based system is employed for both Attendance Confirmation (RSVP) and a digital Guestbook. The adoption of an IEEE-style format serves to provide a structured, academic presentation of this social event, emphasizing the 'materials and methods' of guest management.

RELATED WORK

The management of guest lists has traditionally relied heavily on physical paper invitations and manual tallying. Modern digital approaches, however, allow for efficient confirmation collection and real-time interaction. Studies by social science researchers often explore the sociology of large gatherings [1]. This work builds upon the shift from analogue to digital communication by providing two distinct, integrated web forms. The RSVP form is a critical data collection tool for event planning, while the guestbook form serves as a means for hosts to collect personalized messages and quotes from attendees, capturing qualitative, sentimental data.

MATERIALS AND METHODS

The primary materials used are two distinct interactive web forms implemented in HTML/CSS/JavaScript, designed to collect quantitative (attendance status) and qualitative (guestbook message) data, respectively. Both forms are optimized for rapid input and submission.

A. RSVP / Attendance Form

The attendance confirmation system captures the guest’s identifier (Name, Email) and a binary variable representing their ability to attend the ceremony. This is essential for final catering and seating arrangement planning.

B. Guestbook Form (Quotes / Messages)

The guestbook acts as a data repository for qualitative textual submissions. Guests are prompted to submit a personalized message or quote for the newly married couple.

C. Event Location and Timing

The matrimonial ceremony is defined by the following spatial and temporal parameters:

  • Date: Sunday, 20 July 2025.
  • Reception Time: 12:00 PM (GMT+8) to 4:00 PM (GMT+8).
  • Location: Dewan Seri Harmoni, Kuala Terengganu, Malaysia. The venue was selected for its centralized accessibility and capacity, optimizing the travel time and logistical constraints for the invited population.
D. Attire and Theme Specifications

To ensure high quality visual data collection and a unified aesthetic, the following specifications for guest attire must be adhered to:

  • Dress Code: Formal or Traditional Wear.
  • Theme: White and Gold (Symbolizing Purity and Prosperity).
  • Note: Adherence to this dress code serves as a control variable for the visual aesthetic dataset.

CONCLUSION

The dual-form system successfully implemented within an academic document framework allows for structured attendance confirmation and the capture of meaningful, unsolicited quotes from guests. The structured IEEE-style layout provides an organized and technically formal presentation of a significant social event, validating the utility of web-based forms in event management.
